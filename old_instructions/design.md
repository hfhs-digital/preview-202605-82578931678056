# Higashi-Fukuoka School Festival 2026 — Design Philosophy

> **The site should feel like a pale festival guide made from paper, pattern, and light.**

It should remain airy and hydrangea-like, but it should now express a stronger **Japanese pattern identity** inspired by the official school festival logo.

---

## 1. Source of truth

This file is the primary design authority for the project.

If `origami-execution-plan.md`, `todo.md`, or the current implementation differs from this philosophy, **this document wins**.

---

## 2. Core impression

The site should feel:

- pale,
- elegant,
- paper-based,
- calm,
- and distinctly Japanese in pattern language.

It should not feel:

- metallic,
- corporate,
- generic editorial,
- overly westernized,
- or dependent on photography for atmosphere.

The mood is not “luxury brochure.”  
It is closer to **a refined school festival guide with hydrangea light and traditional pattern discipline**.

---

## 3. Main visual identity: hydrangea ichimatsu

The official logo suggests a clearer direction than the previous interpretation.

The key motif is not a soft badge or emblem.  
It is a **visible Japanese-pattern structure**, especially:

- ichimatsu,
- layered diamond framing,
- paper geometry,
- and hydrangea-like color softness.

### 3.1 Motif character

The pattern should feel:

- geometric,
- orderly,
- paper-based,
- and Japanese in spirit.

It should still remain light and elegant rather than heavy or crowded.

### 3.2 Correct interpretation

The site should use:

- clearer checker structure,
- repeated square / diamond rhythm,
- folded-paper geometry,
- and pale hydrangea tones over white paper.

### 3.3 Wrong interpretation

Avoid treating the identity as:

- a circular emblem,
- a medallion,
- a crest placed above every title,
- or a purely floral decoration with no structural pattern logic.

---

## 4. Title philosophy

The title system should be revised.

Titles should **not** be small and tucked to the upper-left as the main composition.

Instead, major page and section titles should feel closer to the Komaba Festival reference:

- large,
- centered,
- calm,
- and placed prominently in open white space.

### 4.1 Composition

The standard title composition should include:

- a large centered title,
- fine framing lines,
- visible but restrained ichimatsu / diamond accents,
- and generous empty space around the title.

### 4.2 Ornament placement

The pattern may still overlap the title zone slightly, but the overall composition should read as:

> **one large central title framed by Japanese-pattern accents**

rather than:

> **a small title decorated at the corners**

### 4.3 Information density

Keep the title block simple.

Do not default to:

- eyebrow labels,
- description paragraphs,
- emblem rows,
- or extra explanatory copy.

The title should carry the composition by itself.

---

## 5. Color philosophy

The palette should remain pale and hydrangea-based.

However, the pattern language may now be more visible than before.

### 5.1 Core colors

Use:

- paper white,
- hydrangea lavender,
- pale wisteria,
- washed blue-violet,
- misted lilac,
- and small amounts of blush pink.

### 5.2 Ink tone

Dark ink tones may be used for typography and framing lines so the Japanese pattern structure reads clearly.

### 5.3 What to avoid

Do not return to:

- coral-led accents,
- gold-led ceremonial richness,
- or indigo-heavy contrast as the emotional center.

The site should still feel pale first.

---

## 6. Typography

The current font setup is acceptable and should remain.

Typography is **not** the redesign priority.

Do not introduce a large font migration.  
Focus on composition, pattern, color, and paper modules instead.

---

## 7. Page structure

The site should still feel like one broad white paper canvas.

### 7.1 Surface

- keep the overall page white or nearly white,
- use subtle tonal layering instead of hard page resets,
- and let pattern accents appear with intention rather than as wallpaper.

### 7.2 Section rhythm

Sections should be connected by:

- spacing,
- gentle tonal shifts,
- repeated ichimatsu rhythm,
- and small finishing ornaments near section endings.

---

## 8. Content modules

The site should no longer depend on photo-heavy highlights.

For sections such as highlights, featured information, or guide modules, prefer:

- image-free composition,
- folded-paper cards,
- origami memo / note shapes,
- and attached-slip or tag-like panels.

The feeling can be close to:

- a pinned festival note,
- a folded information slip,
- or a paper placard,

but it should remain elegant rather than playful in a childish way.

---

## 9. Motion and loading

Motion should feel like paper being placed, opened, or aligned.

Prefer:

- soft reveal,
- line drawing,
- subtle unfolding,
- and quiet settling.

The loading direction may combine:

- hydrangea light,
- paper folds,
- and geometric ichimatsu rhythm.

---

## 10. Practical decision order

When design decisions are ambiguous, prioritize:

1. `design.md`,
2. pale hydrangea atmosphere,
3. stronger Japanese-pattern ichimatsu structure,
4. large centered titles,
5. image-free folded-paper modules,
6. and calm continuity across the full site.

If an idea is stylish but too photographic, too dark, too decorative without structure, or too small in title presence, it is the wrong direction.

---

## 11. Summary

This project should express:

- white paper,
- pale hydrangea color,
- visible Japanese-pattern structure,
- large centered titles,
- and image-free origami paper modules.

The goal is not to make the site louder.  
It is to make it **more coherent, more Japanese in motif, and more faithful to the festival logo’s visual language**.
