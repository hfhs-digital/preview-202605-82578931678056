# Higashi-Fukuoka School Festival 2026 — Origami Execution Plan

## 1. Role of this document

This file translates `design.md` into practical implementation guidance.

`design.md` is the source of truth.

If this file drifts away from `design.md`, it must be corrected.

---

## 2. Updated direction after reviewing the festival logo

The logo clarified an important point:

the intended atmosphere is not only soft hydrangea light, but also a clearer **Japanese-pattern structure**.

The current direction should therefore be:

- pale hydrangea color,
- white-paper continuity,
- stronger ichimatsu / diamond rhythm,
- larger centered titles,
- and image-free folded-paper content modules.

This replaces the weaker previous idea of:

- mostly corner-only accents,
- smaller title compositions,
- emblem-led decoration,
- and image-dependent highlight sections.

---

## 3. Visual keywords

Design and implementation should consistently reflect:

- hydrangea,
- ichimatsu,
- folded paper,
- diamond framing,
- pinned notes,
- and quiet festival formality.

The site should feel like a real school festival guide rather than a generic modern event website.

---

## 4. Non-negotiable principles

Every implementation choice should preserve the following:

1. **The page remains bright and paper-based.**
2. **The palette stays pale and hydrangea-led.**
3. **Titles become larger and more centered.**
4. **The motif system is structural, not emblematic.**
5. **Content modules do not rely on photography.**
6. **The current font setup remains unchanged.**

---

## 5. Palette guidance

### 5.1 Main palette

Base the interface on:

- paper white,
- hydrangea lavender,
- pale wisteria,
- washed blue-violet,
- misted lilac,
- and small touches of blush pink.

### 5.2 Structural ink

Dark ink tones may be used for:

- title text,
- framing lines,
- and pattern definition.

This is important because the logo communicates its identity through clear structure as much as through softness.

### 5.3 Colors to demote

The following should no longer be the emotional center:

- coral,
- gold,
- and heavy indigo.

They may appear in tiny amounts if needed, but they should not define the site.

---

## 6. Motif system: visible ichimatsu and diamond structure

### 6.1 Main motif

The core motif is now:

- clearer ichimatsu,
- layered squares and diamonds,
- paper-fold geometry,
- and hydrangea softness applied to that structure.

### 6.2 What this means visually

The pattern should read more clearly than before.

It should not disappear into vague floral mist.  
At the same time, it must remain elegant and pale rather than loud.

### 6.3 Wrong direction

Do not build the UI around:

- circular emblems,
- badge-like ornaments,
- medallion symbols,
- or decorative motifs that feel detached from page structure.

### 6.4 Approved usage

Use the pattern system in:

- title frames,
- hero background structure,
- section-ending ornaments,
- panel edges,
- loading and transition graphics,
- and selected large background compositions.

---

## 7. Title system

### 7.1 Main rule

Major page and section titles should now be:

- larger,
- centered,
- and compositionally important.

They should resemble the compositional confidence of the Komaba Festival reference more than the previous smaller corner-led headings.

### 7.2 Default title composition

The standard heading should include:

1. a large centered title,
2. thin framing lines,
3. subtle but visible ichimatsu / diamond accents,
4. optional short subline only when needed.

### 7.3 What to avoid

Do not default to:

- eyebrow labels,
- description paragraphs,
- emblem blocks,
- or multi-row heading structures.

### 7.4 Scale and spacing

Titles should be allowed to breathe.

The composition should rely on:

- open white space,
- calm framing,
- and clear central placement,

rather than many stacked supporting elements.

---

## 8. Content module system: origami notes instead of photos

### 8.1 Main decision

Highlight sections and similar homepage content should no longer assume large photography assets.

Instead, use image-free modules inspired by:

- folded slips,
- pinned notes,
- paper tags,
- and origami memo cards.

### 8.2 Desired feeling

These modules should feel:

- useful,
- crafted,
- slightly dimensional,
- and integrated with the paper world of the site.

They may resemble sticky notes in logic, but should stay refined and festival-guide-like rather than casual office stationery.

### 8.3 Good applications

This system fits:

- highlights,
- visitor guidance,
- featured announcements,
- program summaries,
- and compact news teasers.

### 8.4 Avoid

Avoid:

- generic rectangular card grids,
- photo-first highlight layouts,
- and glass-like floating cards that ignore the new paper language.

---

## 9. Page-wide structure

### 9.1 Canvas

The whole site should continue to behave as one white sheet.

### 9.2 Section continuity

Connect sections through:

- spacing,
- tonal layering,
- repeated pattern rhythm,
- and gentle paper accents near the close of each section.

### 9.3 Section endings

Section endings are now important decorative moments.

Use:

- small ichimatsu fragments,
- folded-paper marks,
- or hydrangea-tinted geometric accents

to gently close one section and lead into the next.

---

## 10. Component-level guidance

### 10.1 Shared heading components

Shared heading components should be rebuilt around:

- large centered titles,
- line framing,
- visible but pale pattern accents,
- and no mandatory extra copy.

### 10.2 Decorative components

Any motif component should evolve away from the previous emblem idea and toward:

- checker fragments,
- diamond overlays,
- folded-corner accents,
- and structural pattern pieces.

### 10.3 Home hero

The hero should:

- keep a curated, calm presence,
- place the title centrally and confidently,
- use the logo direction as a pattern reference,
- and reduce dependence on image-heavy storytelling.

### 10.4 Home lower sections

The lower homepage should be rebuilt with:

- origami note modules,
- pattern-based separators,
- and the same title language as the hero.

### 10.5 Inner pages

News, timetable, map, and CMS pages should use:

- centered titles,
- clearer Japanese-pattern framing,
- and simplified copy structures.

---

## 11. Motion and loading

Motion should feel like:

- paper aligning,
- folds settling,
- and pattern lines resolving into place.

Loading may use:

- hydrangea-tinted geometry,
- diamond / checker emergence,
- and quiet paper-unfold motion.

---

## 12. What to stop doing

Do not continue the previous drift toward:

- emblem-led identity,
- small corner-dominant title compositions,
- photo-first highlight sections,
- overbuilt heading copy,
- or dark editorial contrast.

These are not aligned with the clarified logo-based direction.

---

## 13. Immediate implementation order

1. Strengthen the ichimatsu and diamond motif system across the site.
2. Rebuild title components around large centered composition.
3. Replace image-based highlights with origami note / folded-paper modules.
4. Update section endings to use pattern-led closing accents.
5. Keep the current font setup and pale hydrangea palette.

---

## 14. Final reminder

The site should now be understood as:

> **a pale hydrangea festival guide structured by Japanese pattern and folded paper**

not:

> **a soft site occasionally decorated with floral corner motifs**

That distinction should guide all future implementation decisions.
